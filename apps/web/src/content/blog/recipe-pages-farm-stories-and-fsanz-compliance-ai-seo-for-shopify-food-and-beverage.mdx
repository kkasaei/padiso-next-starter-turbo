## Why Food SEO Is Different

Food shoppers search differently than any other ecommerce category:
- **Recipe queries** — "chocolate cake recipe" leads to ingredient purchases
- **Dietary queries** — "gluten free pasta" targets specific product needs
- **Origin queries** — "Australian olive oil" values provenance
- **Comparison queries** — "almond milk vs oat milk" drives brand discovery

The opportunity: recipe and food education content has massive search volume and naturally leads to product purchases.

## The Recipe Content Strategy

Recipes are the #1 traffic driver for food ecommerce SEO:

### Recipe Page Structure
- Title with SEO keyword: "Easy Sourdough Bread Recipe (Using [Your Brand] Flour)"
- Recipe schema markup (enables rich results with ratings, cook time, calories)
- High-quality step-by-step photos
- Ingredient list with links to your products
- Nutritional information
- Tips and variations section

### AI-Generated Recipe Content
AI can produce recipes at scale:
- Generate 50-100 recipes featuring your products
- Create seasonal recipe collections
- Build dietary-specific recipe guides (vegan, keto, gluten-free)
- Produce "5 ways to use [product]" content for every SKU

### Recipe Schema for Rich Results
Recipe schema gets your pages into Google's rich results carousel:
- `Recipe` schema with all required properties
- `NutritionInformation` for health-conscious shoppers
- `HowTo` steps for visual recipe cards
- `AggregateRating` from user reviews

## Farm-to-Table and Origin Story Content

Provenance content builds E-E-A-T and trust:
- "Meet our [product] producers" — human stories behind your products
- "How [product] is made" — process content with photos and video
- "From farm to shelf: Our supply chain" — transparency content
- Regional focus: "Why Tasmanian honey is different" — local authority content

## FSANZ Compliance for Product Descriptions

Australian food products must comply with FSANZ labelling standards:

- **Allergen declarations** — must be prominently displayed
- **Nutritional information panels** — must match physical label
- **Country of origin** — required labelling
- **Health claims** — must meet FSANZ pre-approved claim requirements
- **Organic claims** — must be certified to claim "organic"

AI content generation can be trained with these requirements to produce compliant product descriptions at scale.

## Technical SEO for Food Stores

### Freshness and Seasonality
- Update seasonal product pages rather than creating new URLs
- Publish timely content (holiday recipes, seasonal ingredient guides)
- Keep product availability and pricing current in schema

### Product Variant Handling
Food products often have many variants (size, flavour, dietary version):
- Canonical tags to prevent duplicate content
- Unique descriptions for variants that serve different search intents
- Schema markup for each variant's nutritional information

## FAQs

- **Can recipe content really drive product sales?**
Absolutely. Recipe pages are the highest-converting content type for food ecommerce. Shoppers who find your brand through a recipe have strong purchase intent.

- **How many recipes should I publish?**
Start with 20-30 featuring your core products. Scale to 100+ over 6 months. Each recipe targets different search queries.

- **Do I need professional food photography?**
It helps significantly for recipe pages. For product descriptions, clean product photos suffice. Some stores successfully use AI-enhanced photography.
