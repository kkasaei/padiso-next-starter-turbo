## Why Black Friday SEO Starts 30 Days Out

Google needs time to crawl, index, and rank new content. If you publish your Black Friday pages on November 28, you've already lost. The winning strategy:

- **Day 1-7**: Create and publish all Black Friday landing pages and content
- **Day 8-14**: Build internal links and submit for indexation
- **Day 15-21**: Monitor rankings and optimize underperforming pages
- **Day 22-30**: Final optimizations, schema updates, and speed checks

## The Black Friday Content Stack

### 1. Black Friday Landing Page
A dedicated `/pages/black-friday` or `/collections/black-friday` page:
- H1 with "Black Friday [Year]" and your brand name
- Preview of deals (updated as deals are finalized)
- Email capture for early access
- Schema markup with Event and Sale properties
- Internal links to all deal collections

### 2. Black Friday Gift Guides
"Black Friday Gift Guide for [Persona]" pages target high-intent queries:
- "Black Friday gifts for her under $100"
- "Best Black Friday tech deals 2025"
- "Black Friday beauty deals Australia"
- Each guide links to specific products on sale

### 3. Deal Category Pages
Dedicated collections for each deal category:
- "Black Friday [Category] Deals"
- 30-50% off collection
- Doorbusters and limited quantities
- Each with unique meta titles and descriptions

### 4. Blog Content
Supporting blog posts that capture informational queries:
- "When does Black Friday start in Australia?"
- "Black Friday vs Cyber Monday: When to buy [category]"
- "How to shop Black Friday online: Tips for the best deals"

## AI-Powered Black Friday Content Production

AI compresses weeks of content creation into days:

- **Generate all gift guide content** in a single session

## Technical Black Friday SEO

### Page Speed Is Critical
Black Friday pages get massive traffic spikes. Ensure:
- All images are optimized and lazy-loaded
- No unnecessary apps or scripts on sale pages
- CDN is configured for traffic surges
- Mobile experience is fast and smooth

### Schema Markup for Sales
Implement sale-specific schema:
- Product schema with `priceValidUntil` for deal expiration
- `Sale` event schema on your main Black Friday page
- Updated `Offer` schema showing sale prices

### Indexation Strategy
- Submit Black Friday pages to Google immediately after publishing
- Add prominent internal links from your homepage and navigation
- Include in your sitemap
- Request indexation via Search Console URL inspection tool

## Post-Black Friday Strategy

Don't delete your Black Friday pages after the sale:

- **Redirect to a "Sale ended" page** with email capture for next year
- **Keep the URL active** to preserve any authority and backlinks earned
- **Update the same pages** next year instead of creating new URLs
- **Repurpose content** for Boxing Day and other seasonal sales

## FAQs

- **Is it too late to start Black Friday SEO 30 days out?**
For competitive head terms, yes. But long-tail queries like "Black Friday [niche product] deals Australia" can rank quickly with AI-optimized content.

- **Should I create separate pages or update existing collection pages?**
Both. Create a dedicated Black Friday hub page and update your regular collection pages with sale messaging and schema.

- **How do I handle products that sell out during the sale?**
Update availability schema in real-time. Redirect sold-out product pages to alternatives. Never show "out of stock" without suggesting similar products.
