## 1. What Is Anti-Commodity SEO?

Anti-commodity SEO is the practice of deliberately breaking away from generic, copycat content strategies and focusing on originality and differentiation. Unlike most SEO approaches that chase after the same keywords and content formats, anti-commodity SEO, echoing Seth Godin’s ideas, forces you to ask: What makes my content worth noticing?

  - It’s about being unique, not just optimized.
  - It’s about memorable brands, not forgettable blog posts.
  - You resist the urge to mass-produce. Instead, you create with purpose.

If the sea of online content is an ocean, average content is seawater. Anti-commodity SEO is the colored dye that makes your spot visible from space.

## 2. How Does Seth Godin Define Commoditization?

Seth Godin argues that commoditization happens when what you make or sell is interchangeable with everything else. There’s no edge, no story, no connection. The only thing left to compete on is price, and that’s a race to the bottom.

**Godin’s anti-commodity stance means:**

- Refusing to be generic.
- Offering distinction—either by design, story, or experience.
- Winning on meaning, not just metrics.

## 3. Why Average Content Fails in Modern SEO

Average content gets buried. Search engines favor content that provides unique value, not regurgitated answers. With the rise of AI Overview and SGE (Search Generative Experience), the winners are not the ones with the most content—they’re the ones with the best, most helpful, and most distinctive answers.

  - Google’s evolving algorithms reward depth and uniqueness.
  - SGE synthesizes from authority—not just quantity.
  - If your answer feels like everyone else’s, expect zero featured snippets.

I’ve seen firsthand: Differentiation turns browsers into buyers, and visitors into subscribers. Seth Godin’s work—especially in *Purple Cow*—argues you have to be remarkable or be invisible. How can you bring that to SEO?

  - Define your unique value proposition and make it the headline.
  - Use voice, tone, format, and even design to break the monotony.

## 5. Building a Seth Godin-Inspired SEO Framework

Let’s build a practical framework, step by step:

  - **Find Your Edge**: What do you know or offer that few others can?
  - **Tell a Story**: Embed narrative and context in your SEO pages.
  - **Challenge Conventions**: Don’t just echo top results. Improve or refute them.
  - **Test Relentlessly**: Use analytics to see what stands out.
  - **Feedback Loops**: Listen to your community. Shift as new insights emerge.

This framework isn’t static. It evolves—like the best brands.

## 6. Unique Value: The Core of Anti-Commodity SEO

Your unique value is your unfair advantage. Seth Godin calls it your "remarkability." In practice, that could mean:

  - Sharing original research.
  - Publishing authentic customer stories.
  - Creating interactive tools or calculators.
  - Giving a more nuanced or honest answer than competitors.

Ask yourself: If this disappeared tomorrow, would anyone miss it? If the answer’s no, it’s time to reimagine your content.

## 8. Anti-Commodity Content Strategy: Practical Steps

My process is simple:

  - Audit your existing content. Highlight sameness.

  - Map competitor content. Pinpoint gaps and missed opportunities.
