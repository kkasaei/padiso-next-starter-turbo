## 1. What Does Seth Godin Mean by 'Make Better Promises'?

Seth Godin’s 'make better promises' philosophy means telling people what you’ll do for them—and actually doing it. He believes trust is currency online. He’s famous for saying, “Don’t promise what you can’t deliver. Promise what you can over-deliver.” In short:

  - Make your value clear.
  - Commit to something unique.
  - Follow through better than the competition.

Example: Ever seen Apple guarantee privacy in every ad? That’s a bold promise they keep. For a dive into unique value, [see our blog post: Unique Value Proposition Guidelines](https://searchfit.ai/waitlist/blog/unique-value-proposition-guidelines).

## 2. Why Do Most Landing Pages Fail to Convert?

Most landing pages ramble about features and forget the promise. People want to know 'what's in it for me?' in seconds. If your page doesn’t answer that, your conversion rate tanks. Compare copy like:

  - 'Our platform is built on leading-edge tech.'
  - vs. 'Never waste another hour searching—find what you need in one click.'

The second one is a clear, actionable promise. It’s the Seth Godin playbook for high-converting landing pages.

## 3. Building a Value Proposition That Ranks

A relatable, search-aligned promise helps you rank. Google (and AI Overviews) favor clear, benefits-based headlines and easy-to-understand language. Steps to craft a value proposition that ranks:

  - Identify your core benefit.
  - Phrase it simply and uniquely.
  - Weave customer pain points into your copy.

Be honest: Would you click a link that simply says ‘Best Platform,' or one that promises 'Double Your Pipeline, Guaranteed'?

## 4. The Seth Godin Checklist for Conversion Copy

Godin suggests a conversion copy checklist:

  - Promise a real outcome.
  - Avoid hype—keep it true.
  - Make the next step easy and obvious.

Test your copy. Would you feel silly if you put your mom’s name under your claim?

## 5. Why Promises Affect SEO and CRO (Not Just Copy)

## 8. Common Mistakes: Broken and Overblown Promises

Here’s what blows up trust fast:

  - Vague claims: 'Best in class!'
  - Promises you can’t keep: '100% uptime forever.'
  - No evidence to support the promise.

If you trip up, own it. Admit your mistake publicly—Godin’s approach builds more trust in the long run.

  - 'How [Audience] Can Get [Desire] Without [Hassle].'

Use these, but always keep the promise real.

## 15. How to Avoid Over-Promising While Still Standing Out

  - Only promise what you can measure.
  - Use social proof or data to back up big claims.
  - Say 'up to X' or 'on average' if results vary.

Better to under-promise and wow them later.

## 16. Emotional vs. Rational Promises: Balancing the Two

Some industries thrive on emotion (think Nike: 'Just Do It'). Others require logic (like SaaS: 'Cut costs by 24%' is persuasive). Great value propositions blend both—'Transform your workflow *and* have more time for family.'
