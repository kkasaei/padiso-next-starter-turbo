## 1. What Is The Dip According to Seth Godin—and Why Does It Matter for SEO?

Seth Godin describes The Dip as the tough stretch between starting something new and achieving breakthrough success. Almost every worthwhile pursuit has a Dip: it’s that slog when progress seems slow, resources are stretched, and quick wins dry up.

- For SEOs, The Dip is when tried-and-true tactics stop working.
- Your content isn’t ranking like it used to.
- Google introduces SGE, AI rewrites SERPs, and suddenly, what got you here won’t get you to the next level.

Recognizing you’re in The Dip is crucial. Stick with the wrong tactics, and you waste time. Pivot too soon, and you never break through. I’ll show you how Seth’s concept gives you the language to make smarter, more strategic choices.

## 2. The Classic SEO Dip—And Why It’s Hitting Harder in the AI Era

Remember when link-building and keyword stuffing felt like gold? Or when just having a blog was enough to win traffic?

- Those were the early wins—before The Dip set in.
- Now, every minor Google algorithm tweak leaves those old playbooks obsolete.
- With Emergent tech like SGE and LLMs (Large Language Models), the goalposts keep moving.

The Dip today is deeper and more deceptive, thanks to constant change. Recognizing this early saves you from doubling down on sunk costs.

## 3. SGE, LLM SEO, and the Unstoppable Rise of AI-Native Strategies

With Google’s SGE (Search Generative Experience), the search landscape is AI-driven. Instead of classic top-10 blue links, users see AI summaries scraping the best answers instantly.

- LLM SEO is about adapting content for AI summarizers, not just humans.
- Structured data, semantic depth, and context matter more than ever.
- Traditional ranking factors take a back seat to AI comprehension.

If you’re not designing content for AI consumption, you’re in an SEO Dip with no way out.

## 4. How to Identify You’re in The SEO Dip (And Not Just Having a Bad Month)

It’s tempting to blame drops in traffic on seasonality or competitors. But here’s how you know it’s The Dip:

- Effort isn’t improving outcomes.
- New tactics aren’t moving the needle.
- Market or platform changes (like SGE) have fundamentally shifted user behavior.

Take a step back. Are you facing normal turbulence or is the game itself changing?

## 5. When to Quit: Seth Godin’s Advice for Strategic Quitting in SEO

Seth Godin says: Strategic quitting is about freeing up your energy for the right opportunity, not quitting at the first sign of trouble.

Here’s when to quit outdated SEO tactics:

- Your core methods are no longer cost-effective.
- Your audience is finding answers somewhere else.
- You’re investing more and more with diminishing returns.

The hard part: quitting feels like failure. In reality, it’s how you make space for your next big win.

## 6. How to Evaluate Your SEO Strategy Through The Dip Lens

I run every major SEO decision through a “Dip Test”:

- Would continuing this tactic give me leverage over the competition if I break through?
- Am I facing a temporary setback or a structural shift in the market?
- Is my resource allocation stuck in the past, or am I investing for emerging trends?

If the answer is “no” to most, it’s time for a pivot.

## 7. AI-Native Pivot: What It Means and Why It Matters for Longevity in SEO

Going AI-native means creating content and site structures with the primary audience being search engines powered by large language models—not just humans or old-school crawlers.

- Write clearly, concisely, and answer questions directly.
- Use schema for structured context.
- Optimize for featured snippets and AI answers, not just rankings.

This pivot is fundamental: an old strategy on a new playing field can’t win.

- Cut loose the 80% of tactics producing 20% (or less) of your results.
