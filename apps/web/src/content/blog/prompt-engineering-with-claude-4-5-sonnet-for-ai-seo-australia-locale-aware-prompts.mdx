## Why Generic AI Content Fails in Australia

I've audited hundreds of AI-generated pages targeting Australian consumers. The same mistakes appear repeatedly:

- **American spelling**—"color" instead of "colour," "optimize" instead of "optimise"
- **Wrong seasons**—referencing "summer sales" in June (it's winter in Australia)
- **Irrelevant cultural references**—mentioning Black Friday as the biggest shopping event (it's growing, but Boxing Day and Click Frenzy are bigger)
- **Missing regulatory context**—no ACCC compliance, no Australian consumer guarantees
- **American pricing assumptions**—referencing USD when the audience thinks in AUD

These errors destroy trust. And trust is what Google's E-E-A-T framework measures.

## The Locale-Aware Prompt Framework

Every prompt for Australian content should include five components:

### 1. Geographic Context
"You are writing for an Australian audience. Use Australian English spelling (colour, optimise, behaviour). Reference Australian cities, brands, and cultural touchpoints."

### 2. Seasonal Awareness
"The Australian seasons are: Summer (December-February), Autumn (March-May), Winter (June-August), Spring (September-November). Reference seasons correctly."

### 3. Regulatory Context
"Content must comply with ACCC (Australian Competition and Consumer Commission) guidelines. Do not make unsubstantiated claims. Include Australian Consumer Law references where relevant."

### 4. Cultural Tone
"Write in a friendly, straightforward tone that resonates with Australian consumers. Avoid overly formal language. Use Australian colloquialisms naturally but don't overdo it."

### 5. Commercial Context
"Reference prices in AUD. Mention Australian shipping options and timeframes. Reference local payment methods (Afterpay, Zip Pay). Include GST-inclusive pricing."

## Prompt Templates for Key Content Types

### Product Descriptions for Australian Shoppers

"Write a product description for [product name] targeting Australian online shoppers. Use Australian English. Reference Australian shipping (Express Post, Australia Post). Mention Afterpay availability. Include sizing in Australian standards. Price is [AUD amount] including GST. Target keyword: [keyword]. Keep to 200-300 words."

### Collection Page Content for Australian SEO

"Write a 1,000-word buying guide for [product category] targeting Australian consumers searching for '[keyword]'. Include: seasonal relevance for the current Australian season, Australian consumer guarantee rights, comparison of brands popular in Australia, and FAQ section addressing common Australian shopper questions (shipping times, returns policy, sizing)."

### Blog Posts for Australian Search Intent

"Write a 2,500-word blog post titled '[title]' for Australian ecommerce shoppers. Structure: introduction addressing the Australian market specifically, 5-7 H2 sections with practical advice, comparison section relevant to Australian brands and retailers, FAQ section with 5 questions Australian shoppers commonly ask, conclusion with CTA. Use Australian English throughout."

## Industry-Specific Australian Prompts

### Health and Beauty (AHPRA/TGA Regulated)
"Content must comply with AHPRA advertising guidelines. Do not make therapeutic claims about cosmetic products. Reference TGA-approved claims only. Include necessary disclaimers for health-related products."

### Financial Products (ASIC Regulated)
"Include relevant ASIC disclaimers. Reference Australian financial regulations. Use AFS licence numbers where applicable. Do not provide specific financial advice."

### Food and Beverage
"Reference Australian food standards (FSANZ). Include Australian nutritional labelling requirements. Mention allergen declarations as required by Australian law."

## Testing Your Australian Content

After generating content with these prompts:

- **Spell check with Australian English dictionary**—catch any American spellings
- **Season verification**—ensure seasonal references match Southern Hemisphere
- **Cultural review**—have an Australian team member review for authenticity
- **Regulatory compliance check**—verify claims against ACCC guidelines
- **Local SEO check**—ensure Australian-specific search terms are present

## Measuring Australian Content Performance

Track Australia-specific metrics:

- **Organic traffic from Australia** in GA4 (filter by country)
- **Keyword rankings in google.com.au**—not google.com
- **Conversion rate for Australian visitors**—vs international visitors
- **Bounce rate by country**—high Australian bounce rate suggests content doesn't resonate

## FAQs

- **Can Claude 4.5 Sonnet write in Australian slang?**
Yes, but be specific in your prompt. "Use Australian colloquialisms naturally" produces better results than "write in Australian slang."

- **How do I handle content for both Australian and international audiences?**
Create separate content for each market. Don't try to make one piece work for everyone—it will resonate with no one.

- **Is it worth creating Australia-specific content if we're a global brand?**
Absolutely. Australian consumers can tell when content wasn't written for them. Localised content converts significantly better.

- **Can I use the same prompts for New Zealand?**
New Zealand has its own spelling preferences, cultural context, and regulations (Commerce Commission, not ACCC). Create separate prompts.
