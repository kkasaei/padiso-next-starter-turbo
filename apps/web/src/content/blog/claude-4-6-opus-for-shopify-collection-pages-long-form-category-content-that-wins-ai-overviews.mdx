## The Collection Page Content Framework

Every high-performing Shopify collection page follows this structure:

### Above the fold:
- **H1 heading** with primary keyword
- **Introduction paragraph** (100-150 words) addressing search intent directly
- **Quick navigation** links to subcategories or buying guide sections

### Below the product grid:
- **Buying guide section** (400-600 words) with H2/H3 headings
- **Comparison or "how to choose" content**
- **FAQ section** with schema markup
- **Related collections** internal links

Claude 4.6 Opus generates all of this from a single prompt, using your product data as context.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Don't hide content below an infinite scroll**—Google needs to crawl it
- **Don't duplicate content across similar collections**—each page needs unique editorial content
- **Don't stuff keywords into headings**—Claude naturally integrates keywords; trust the output
- **Don't forget mobile**—ensure long-form content reads well on mobile devices with proper formatting

## FAQs

- **Will long-form content on collection pages hurt page speed?**
Text is lightweight. The content itself won't impact Core Web Vitals. Heavy images and scripts are the real culprits.

- **How often should I update collection page content?**
Quarterly, or whenever you significantly change the products in a collection. Seasonal collections should be updated for each season.

- **Can Claude 4.6 Opus write collection content in multiple languages?**
Yes—its multilingual capabilities are strong. Generate locale-specific content for each Shopify Market.

- **Should I put editorial content above or below the product grid?**
Introduction above, detailed buying guide below. This gives both users and Google what they need.
