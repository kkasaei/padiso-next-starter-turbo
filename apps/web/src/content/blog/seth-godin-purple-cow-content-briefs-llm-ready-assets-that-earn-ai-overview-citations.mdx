## 1. What Is a Purple Cow Content Brief?

A **Purple Cow content brief** is inspired by Seth Godin’s classic idea: Be remarkable in a sea of sameness. In content, this means building briefs that help writers produce content that is not just good—but different in a way that matters.

 - **Unique angles** that others miss
 - Actionable insights, not generic tips
 - **Specific audience targeting**
 - Comprehensive, yet skimmable structure

For more strategies on how to differentiate your editorial process, [see our blog post: Editorial Briefs vs Content Briefs: Which is Better?](https://searchfit.ai/waitlist/blog/editorial-briefs-vs-content-briefs-which-better)

## 2. Seth Godin’s Philosophy and Content Innovation

Seth Godin’s **Purple Cow** is about standing out. He asks: "Are you remarkable or just invisible?" That’s a question every content strategist should ponder.

 - Godin’s core principle: Solve unique reader pain points
 - Make your content worth talking about—and sharing
 - Turn every brief into a springboard for creativity

I apply Godin’s approach to SEO briefs by asking: "Would I bookmark this myself or send it to a friend?" If the answer is no, the brief needs more "purple."

## 3. Why LLM-Ready Content Matters for SEO

Most traditional SEO focuses on keyword rankings. LLM-ready (Large Language Model) assets target AI-driven summaries as well. If you want your content cited in AI Overviews, it must:

 - **Directly answer** real user questions
 - Be structured for machine understanding—not just humans
 - Deliver accurate, succinct facts

This is crucial for both search engine visibility and appearing in AI-generated content digests.

## 4. Building Content Briefs for AI Overview Citations

An AI Overview citation is the new "position zero." Here’s how your content briefs become citation magnets:

 - **Clear, question-based H2s and H3s**
 - Concise intro sentences that answer the search intent, fast
 - Well-tagged data—tables, lists, definitions
 - Original expert quotes or fresh viewpoints

Special tip: Use language that’s literal and explicit. AI models love directness.

## 9. What Makes Content "LLM-Ready"?

 - Clear structure: Logical, skimmable, well-tagged (H1-H4s, lists, tables)
 - Concise and explicit: One idea per paragraph
 - Author authority: Add unique data or voice
 - Transparent citations: Link to trustworthy sources

Models reward content that’s both easy to parse and useful to real users.

## 10. Purple Cow Content Briefs vs. Traditional Content Briefs

Here's how they differ:

 - **Purple Cow:** Risk-taking, calls out the old assumptions, injects personality
 - **Traditional:** Formulaic, keyword-stuffed, bland, easily forgotten

Ask yourself: "Would a competitor’s content look exactly the same?" If yes, make it more purple.

## 11. From Brief to AI Citation: The Journey

LLMs love content that’s:

 - Rich in context, not just facts
 - Organized for quick lookup (FAQs, tables, lists)
 - Free of ambiguity and filler

Brief → Unique, structured draft → Published authority → Cited in AI outputs. That’s the playbook.

## 12. Avoiding Common Mistakes in Purple Cow Briefs

Here are traps I see folks fall into:

 - Pretending to be different, but really just reshuffling old ideas
 - Forgetting to address the real target audience
 - Ignoring current LLM formatting best practices
 - Skipping fact checks

Build in a QA step. It makes a huge difference.
