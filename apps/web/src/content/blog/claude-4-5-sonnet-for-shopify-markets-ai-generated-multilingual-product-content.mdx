## The International SEO Problem on Shopify

When you enable Shopify Markets, you get multiple storefronts for different countries and languages. But the SEO challenges stack up fast:

- **Duplicate content**—the same English descriptions appear on your .com, .co.uk, and .com.au stores
- **Poor translations**—machine translation produces awkward, unconvincing product copy
- **Hreflang chaos**—incorrect or missing hreflang tags cause cannibalization between locales
- **Local intent mismatch**—Australian shoppers search differently than American or German shoppers
- **Currency and sizing confusion**—products reference wrong units for the target market

## The Multilingual Content Workflow

### 1. Define Your Market Strategy

Before generating content, map out:
- Which markets are you targeting? (US, UK, Australia, Germany, France, Japan, etc.)
- What language variations do you need? (US English vs UK English vs Australian English)
- What are the priority product categories for each market?
- What local SEO keywords matter in each market?

### 2. Generate Locale-Specific Content with Claude 4.5 Sonnet

For each market, generate:
- **Product descriptions**—localized, not translated, with local keywords
- **Collection page content**—addressing local shopping behaviors and preferences
- **Meta titles and descriptions**—optimized for local search patterns
- **FAQ content**—addressing market-specific questions (shipping times, duties, returns)

### 3. Implement Proper Hreflang Tags

Hreflang tells Google which version of a page to show in each market. Get it right:

- Every page must have hreflang tags pointing to all its locale variants
- Include a self-referencing hreflang tag
- Use correct language-country codes (en-US, en-AU, de-DE, fr-FR)
- Include an x-default tag for users outside your target markets

Claude 4.5 Sonnet can generate the complete hreflang tag set for your store as a Liquid snippet.

## Market-Specific Content Strategies

### Australia
- Use Australian English spelling and slang
- Reference local seasons (summer = December-February)
- Include AUD pricing and local shipping information
- Address ACCC compliance requirements
- Optimize for ".com.au" search patterns

### United Kingdom
- British English spelling and terminology
- Reference UK sizing standards
- Include GBP pricing and Royal Mail shipping details
- GDPR-compliant content and cookie notices
- Optimize for ".co.uk" search patterns

### Germany
- Formal German product descriptions
- Metric measurements exclusively
- Strict compliance with German consumer protection laws
- Include VAT-inclusive pricing
- German-specific SEO keywords (not just translated English keywords)

## Technical Shopify Implementation

### URL Structure
- Subdirectories are recommended: `/en-us/`, `/en-au/`, `/de-de/`
- Ensure Shopify Markets generates clean, crawlable URLs
- Avoid parameter-based locale switching

### Sitemap Configuration
- Generate locale-specific sitemaps
- Include hreflang annotations in sitemap XML
- Submit all locale sitemaps to respective Search Console properties

### Canonical Tags
- Each locale should self-canonicalize (not point to the primary market)
- Never canonical a German page to an English page
