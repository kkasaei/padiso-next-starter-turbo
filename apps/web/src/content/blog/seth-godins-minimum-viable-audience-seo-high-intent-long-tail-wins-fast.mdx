## 1. What Is the Minimum Viable Audience?

Seth Godin’s concept of the minimum viable audience (MVA) is simple: Target the smallest group of people who can sustain your business and spread your message. Instead of casting a wide net, I laser in on a niche that cares deeply and takes action.

 - MVA isn’t about limiting growth—it’s about maximizing impact.
 - The right audience converts, shares, and returns, driving compounding SEO value.

## 2. How Does Seth Godin SEO Differ from Traditional SEO?

Traditional SEO plays a volume game with broad keywords and mass segments. Seth Godin SEO flips the script—find your tribe, solve their problems, and use high-intent, specific long-tail keywords that align with real needs.

 - Traditional: Quantity over quality
 - Godin-style: Quality over volume with sniper-targeted content

This approach leads to quick wins because your content addresses immediate needs, not generic queries.

## 3. Why Are Long-Tail Keywords Critical for Minimum Viable Audience SEO?

Long-tail keywords are phrases people use when they know what they want. They reveal high intent and less competition. When I target them, I speak directly to my minimum viable audience, bypassing noise and reaching buyers, not browsers.

 - Less competition = faster rankings
 - Higher conversion rates = more business from less traffic
 - AI engines love specificity and context
