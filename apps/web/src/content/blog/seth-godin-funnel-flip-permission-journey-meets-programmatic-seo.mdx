## What is the Seth Godin Funnel Flip?

The funnel flip turns the classic marketing funnel on its head. Godin argues that rather than pushing everyone through a rigid step-by-step process, modern marketers need to invite audiences into a permission journey. Instead of shouting for attention, you build a system where people feel welcome and choose to engage. This subtle shift makes a world of difference, especially with programmatic SEO and automation. It’s about earning trust upfront rather than extracting value after the fact.

## How the Permission Journey Drives Engagement

Picture the permission journey as an ongoing conversation instead of a forced march. Instead of bombarding visitors with aggressive CTAs, you ask for small permissions along the way—an email, a follow, or simply a click. Each micro-yes builds a stronger bond. When paired with programmatic SEO, the journey gets even smarter—tailored touchpoints for every intent cluster and need. ## Intent Clusters: Organizing Your SEO for Real Buyer Signals

Intent clusters take traditional keyword research to a new level. By grouping related search intents, you can create pathways that match where prospects are in their journey. For example:

- Someone searching “best CRM for startups” is at a different stage than someone searching “CRM churn reduction strategies.”
- Each intent gets a tailor-made entry point—maximizing relevance and boosting permission rates.
